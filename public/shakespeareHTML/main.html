Actus Primus

Scena Prima

Thunder and Lightning. Enter three Witches.

FIRST WITCH
When ſhall we three meet againe?
In Thunder, Lightning, or in Raine?1
SECOND WITCH
When the Hurley-burley's commotion done,
When the Battaile's loſt and wonne.
THIRD WITCH
That will be ere the ſet of Sunne. 5
FIRST WITCH
Where the place?
SECOND WITCH
Vpon the heath. Barren wilderness
THIRD WITCH
There to meet with Macbeth.
FIRST WITCH
I come, Gray-Malkin.2
ALL
Padock calls anon: faire is foule, and foule is fair, 10
Houer through fogge and filthie ayre.3

Exeunt

1 - In later editions, these lines had their punctuation changed, with the first interragotive mark left out: "When shall we three meet again/In thunder, lightning, or in rain?"

2 - Gray-Malkin (or graymalkin) is an affectionate name for a gray cat. In the Middle Ages and Renaissance, it was believed that witches had demons or evil spirits who appeared in the form of an animal to help them perform evil deeds. The First Witch here has a cat familiar, the second a toad (Padock), and later it is revealed the Third Witch has a harpy. In Sir Reginald Scot's book "The Discoverie of Witchcraft" from 1584 (which Shakespeare likely used as a source of inspiration), he describes the powers of witches: "Some say they can keepe divels and spirits in the likenesse of todes and cats."(Book I, Chapter IV)

3- Most consider this piece of dialogue a folio mistake - the majority of later versions give the line of "Padock calls." to the second witch, and "Anon." to the third, and then all three recite the last part together.
